CULTURE & MUST-DO

  • St. Mark's Basilica: It is free to enter, but the queue is always long. I highly recommend booking a priority ticket online to save time.

  • Doge's Palace: A masterpiece of Gothic architecture. Ensure you book online in advance.

  • Rialto Market: For the most authentic experience, visit before 10:00 AM. Note that there is no fish market on Mondays.

  • Gondola: The official rates are €90 by day and €110 by night (for 30 minutes). No need to bargain, but I suggest avoiding the crowded areas around St. Mark's for a more peaceful ride.



HIDDEN GEMS

  • Squero di San Trovaso: One of the last remaining boatyards where gondolas are still built and repaired.

  • Ponte Chiodo: Located in Cannaregio, it is one of the only two bridges in Venice without side parapets.

  • The Islands: Take a waterbus to Burano for its colorful houses and Torcello for its ancient ruins.



EATING & DRINKING

  • Alla Staffa: A cozy, home-style osteria. Perfect for a traditional meal (closed Tuesdays).

  • Al Portego: Great for "cicchetti" (Venetian tapas) and huge portions. Reservations are essential.

  • Gelato: For the best treats, head to Gallonetto or SuSo. If you are near the Zattere, try the famous "Gianduiotto" at Gelato Nico.
